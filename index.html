Prep Questions

  1. Why is visualization effective?

    Most people grasp the truth of an assesment only when they unequivocally envision the evidence for it, something that our kludgy brains alone often can't do well.

  2. What are the more effective ways to encode data for perceptual tasks?

    If you wish to create a successful chart, you need to construct it based on elementary tasks "as high in the hierarchy as possible."
    Tips:
    1. Multiple graphic forms may enable multipletasks
    2. Always plot your data directly
    3. If the difference matters, plot the difference
    4. Use logical and meaningful baselines
    5. If needed, plot your data in several charts with dissimilar scales

  3. What are trade-offs that Cairo asks us to consider when building a visualization?

    1. Methods of encoding on the bottom half of the scale may be appropiate when the goal isn't to enable accurate judgements but to reveal general patterns.
    2. You cannot apply anyone's method of choosing graphic forms uncritically.
